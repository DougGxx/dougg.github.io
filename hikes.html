Camelback Mountain Cholla Trail

The Cholla Trail trailhead is located at 5150 N. Invergordon Road. There is a bathroom, water drinking fountain, sinks and bike racks available. There are no designated parking spots at the trailhead, and limited street parking is available near the trailhead. Hikers are required to parallel park in specific, designated sections along the west side of Invergordon Road. Once parked, there is a gravel path that parallels the south side of Cholla Lane and leads to the trailhead. Multiple spots painted with a yellow curb are designated drop-off areas for ride shares. The trail traverses the eastern slope or "hump" of Camelback Mountain and is 1.5 miles in length. Both trails have been rated as difficult since it can be very steep at times. This hike is around 1.5 miles in length.

McDowell Sonoran Preserve Gateway Loop Trail

The Gateway Loop Trail is located in the Gateway entrance to the McDowell Sonoran Preserve. The Gateway entrance is at 18333 N. Thompson Peak Pkwy. Scottsdale, AZ. Parking, restrooms, shade ramadas, water, an accessible Bajada Nature Trail and interpretive signage. Follow the main trail from the parking area to the Gateway Loop trail. Turn left to begin the loop. Turn right at the junction with the Windgate Pass trail. Follow the Gateway Loop trail over the Gateway Saddle viewpoint to the junction with the Bell Pass trail. Turn right and stay on the Gateway Loop trail all the way back to the Saguaro trail junction, bearing right at a number of trail junctions. Turn left at the Saguaro trail and go westward back to the parking area. This hike is around 4.5 miles in length.

Piestewa Peak Summit Trail

The Piestewa Peak Summit Trail is located in Piestewa Peak Park. There is limited parking in the park 2701 Piestewa Peak Dr, Phoenix, AZ. Picnic tables are available and dogs are allowed. Piestewa Peak Summit Trail is a popular but challenging hike with steep terrain, rocky footing, and rocky stairs, providing a great workout with stunning views of the Phoenix area. Recent reviews mention heavy foot traffic, especially on weekends, and recommend going early to avoid crowds. The trail is well-marked, with most people friendly and encouraging. Sturdy hiking shoes with good traction are necessary due to the rocky terrain. Some parts of the trail involve boulders and scrambles towards the peak. Sunset or sunrise hikes are recommended for beautiful views. The trail typically has heavy traffic, rock stairs, and offers a challenging workout with incredible panoramic views. In March, the area may experience mild temperatures, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities. The length of this trail is 1.2 miles.
